Travelling to Kea

Kea is reached by ferry from Lavrio port, about a 30-minute taxi ride from Athens International Airport. The crossing takes approximately one hour; tickets can be booked via ferryhopper.com.

We recommend renting a car — either at Athens Airport or on arrival at the port — as it is the easiest way to get around the island.

Accommodation

Kea offers a variety of accommodation options, from hotels to private houses available on Booking and Airbnb. Most of the hotels are located either at the port (Korissia) or in Ioulida (the main village, also known as Chora). Alternatively, many lovely houses are available to rent in the area between Ioulida and the wedding venue.

We recommend booking accommodation early, as availability on Kea can be limited on weekends. Please let us know if you’d like help or recommendations.

What to do in Kea?

Kea is a hidden gem and remains less touristic than other islands. We recommend staying around 4-5 days to fully enjoy what the island has to offer, among others: In the Cyclades, "Hora" (Χώρα or Chora) is the traditional name for the main town or capital of an island. Historically, the Hora was often built inland or on a hill for protection from pirates.

  • Ancient footpaths, where many of them lead to the beautiful ruins of Ancient Karthaia.
  • Beautiful beaches (most of them accessed via a dirt road): Sykamia, Kampi, Spathi, Xyla, Kalidonychi.
  • Recommended restaurants: To steki, Ennea Kores, To Perasma, To Kampi, I strofi tou Mimi, Filippas, Rolandos, Piatsa
  • Visit Ioulida (also known as Chora) and enjoy sunset views from above
  • For those seeking extra adventure, explore keaterraactive.com. For e-bike and hiking tours, contact Alex at +306970051121 and mention that you are our wedding guests to receive a better rate.

Other Cycladic islands

Syros The capital of the Cyclades with a beautiful Venetian town (one of the few with direct connections to Kea — there is a boat arriving from Syros to Kea on Thursday 10th of September) Serifos A relaxed classic Cycladic style island with dry, crystal clear beaches and a beautiful traditional chora. Sifnos Great cuisine with more elevated options for food. Milos Spectacular beaches. Kimolos Very small, great for relaxation. Koufonisia Tiny islands with stunning turquoise waters, beautiful beaches, and a laid-back atmosphere. Astypalaia A charming island with a picturesque chora and a relaxed vibe. It also has an airport, making it a convenient option if you’d rather avoid long ferry journeys.

Visiting other islands from Kea

Unfortunately, Kea has limited connections with other islands, as most islands are accessed via the Piraeus port. You will either have to go from Lavrio to Piraeus and then take another ferry, or a faster option is to go from Lavrio to the airport and fly to another island (and avoid extremely long and expensive ferry routes).
